Engagement Process

From claim file
to courtroom record.

Five stages. Documented at every step. The same workflow whether the matter resolves at mediation or proceeds through trial.

Five stages. One continuous record.

PER CASE
01
Stage of Five
Intake & Conflict

Conflict-checked before the file is opened.

An engagement begins with a confidential conversation. Names, parties, and matter type are screened against active and historical engagements before any documents are accepted.

  • A.

    Initial counsel call — case summary, parties, jurisdiction, posture.

  • B.

    Conflict check against active and recent engagement record.

  • C.

    Scope-of-work definition and engagement letter.

  • D.

    Document intake protocol and secure transfer.

Deliverable

Engagement letter with scope of work, fee structure, and document protocol.

Intake — Document
02
Stage of Five
Document Review

Read before walking the site.

Plans, specs, prior reports, claim file, and policy language are reviewed before the first field visit. The site investigation tests hypotheses formed in document review — not the other way around.

  • A.

    Architectural and structural drawings — original construction and any subsequent alterations.

  • B.

    Prior inspection reports, adjuster estimates, third-party Loss Analysis.

  • C.

    Claim file, policy provisions, declarations relevant to scope.

  • D.

    Pre-investigation hypothesis matrix prepared for field testing.

DOC
Deliverable

Internal investigation plan keyed to the documentary record.

Stage of Five
Field Investigation

The site is the witness. Every observation is recorded.

Field investigation operates under documented protocols. Photography, sampling, and measurement proceed under chain-of-custody from the moment of arrival until the moment the report file is closed.

BDocumented sample take
CMeasurement & mapping

04
Stage of Five
Expert Report

The report is the last step, not the first.

OEC and Loss Analysis. Opinions separated from methodology. Basis disclosed. Built for Rule 26 disclosure and Daubert challenge — and built for the client to read in twenty minutes if needed.

  • A.

    Causation analysis — peril, date of loss, building system.

  • B.

    Scope of repair tied to documented field conditions.

  • C.

    Construction cost analysis with regional basis.

  • D.

    Photographic exhibits, schematics, demonstratives.

  • E.

    Statement of opinions and Rule 26 disclosure package.

RPT
Deliverable

Signed expert report (OEC and/or Loss Analysis) with full Rule 26 disclosure.

05
Stage of Five
Deposition & Trial

The opinion has to hold under cross.

Counsel consultation, demonstrative preparation, deposition readiness, and trial testimony — all built off the same evidentiary record assembled in stages 01–04. Nothing is added late.

  • A.

    Pre-deposition counsel meeting and document orientation.

  • B.

    Demonstrative exhibits — photo boards, scope tables, diagram callouts.

  • C.

    Daubert and qualification challenge response support.

  • D.

    Trial testimony — direct, cross, redirect.

The Standards We Hold

The discipline behind the deliverable.

Standards we apply to every engagement — and a few practices we decline.

01 — Standard

Methodology before opinion.

Methodology is selected before conclusions are reached. The basis is documented in the report, not assumed.

02 — Standard

Field record under chain-of-custody.

Photographs, samples, and measurements are documented at the moment of collection — not reconstructed later.

03 — Standard

Independent of outcome.

The fee structure does not change based on the conclusion reached or the resolution of the matter.

04 — Standard

One file, retained for record.

The complete engagement record — every photo, note, communication — is preserved and produced on request.
