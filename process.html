Engagement Process

What happens after you retain us.

Six stages, mapped to the calendar. What counsel receives, when, and in what form — from the conflict check on day one to trial testimony on the back end.

Every engagement runs through the same six stages. Timing shifts with matter size, record volume, and site access — but the sequence does not. Each stage produces a specific deliverable to retaining counsel.

This page is about the calendar. For the philosophy behind the work — why opinions are structured the way they are — see Approach.

01

Conflict & Intake Review

Confidential case screening, scope definition, and engagement terms — aligned to statute-of-limitations pressure when needed.

  • Conflict check against prior engagements
  • Written scope and rate schedule
  • Protective-order compliance confirmed
Timing24–48 hours from initial contact
02

Document Review

Plans, specifications, submittals, RFIs, change orders, inspection records, and prior reports — evaluated before the first site visit so the site work is targeted, not exploratory.

  • Specification and submittal register
  • Prior-expert report review (if any)
  • Site-visit protocol drafted in advance
Timing1–3 weeks depending on volume
03

Field Investigation

Non-destructive and destructive testing under documented protocols. Photography, sampling, and measurements preserved under chain-of-custody.

  • Moisture mapping, infrared, water testing per ASTM
  • Destructive openings with signed logs
  • Opposing counsel notice and joint inspection where appropriate
Timing1–5 days on site; reconvenes for destructive work
04

Root-Cause Analysis

Evidence is mapped against the published standards and the building code in effect at time of construction. Findings memo issued to counsel for review before reporting begins.

  • Findings memo (counsel-only, not for disclosure)
  • Standard and code citations table
  • Alternative-cause matrix
Timing2–6 weeks, overlapping with testing
05

Expert Report Issued

Counsel receives the draft expert report for review, then the final report packaged for Rule 26 or state-equivalent disclosure. Demonstrative exhibits and underlying-data appendices included.

  • Draft report to counsel — comments incorporated
  • Final report with demonstratives and appendices
  • Disclosure-ready packaging (Rule 26 / FRCP)
Timing2–4 weeks after analysis
06

Deposition & Trial

Pre-deposition prep sessions with retaining counsel. Demonstratives produced for mediation or trial. Rebuttal reports issued as opposing experts disclose. Live testimony at deposition and trial.

  • Pre-deposition prep sessions (typically two, scheduled to counsel's calendar)
  • Demonstratives — boards or animations, as the venue requires
  • Rebuttal report on opposing expert disclosure
  • Deposition and trial testimony
TimingDisclosure deadline through verdict; rush accommodated

Rush engagements

Statute-of-limitations matters, pre-suit investigation, and inspection deadlines can compress this timeline. Stages 1–3 can run concurrently when the matter requires it. Note urgency at intake — we'll tell you in 24 hours whether it can be staffed without compromising the documentation chain.
