Engagement Process

A structured, defensible workflow.

Every stage is documented. Every conclusion is traceable. The process itself is what makes the eventual opinion hold.

Every engagement runs through the same six stages. The timing shifts with the matter, but the structure does not — because the structure is what gives the eventual opinion its weight.

01

Conflict & Intake Review

Confidential case screening, scope definition, and engagement terms — aligned to statute-of-limitations pressure when needed.

  • Conflict check against prior engagements
  • Written scope and rate schedule
  • Protective-order compliance confirmed
Timing24–48 hours from initial contact
02

Document Review

Plans, specifications, submittals, RFIs, change orders, inspection records, and prior reports — evaluated before the first site visit so the site work is targeted, not exploratory.

  • Specification and submittal register
  • Prior-expert report review (if any)
  • Site-visit protocol drafted in advance
Timing1–3 weeks depending on volume
03

Field Investigation

Non-destructive and destructive testing under documented protocols. Photography, sampling, and measurements preserved under chain-of-custody.

  • Moisture mapping, infrared, water testing per ASTM
  • Destructive openings with signed logs
  • Opposing counsel notice and joint inspection where appropriate
Timing1–5 days on site; reconvenes for destructive work
04

Root-Cause Analysis

Evidence evaluated against building science, published standards, and the applicable standard of care at time of construction.

  • Citations to ASTM / IIBEC / AAMA / code provisions
  • Alternative-cause analysis documented
  • Basis for each opinion traced to specific evidence
Timing2–6 weeks, overlapping with testing
05

Expert Reporting

Written opinions structured for clarity and admissibility. Opinions separated from methodology. Basis disclosed. Prepared for Rule 26 disclosure.

  • Rule 26 / FRCP-compliant structure
  • Demonstrative exhibits referenced inline
  • Appendices for underlying data, photos, logs
Timing2–4 weeks after analysis
06

Deposition & Trial Support

Ongoing counsel consultation, demonstrative preparation, deposition readiness, and expert testimony through trial.

  • Pre-deposition prep with retaining counsel
  • Demonstratives for mediation or trial
  • Rebuttal reports as the record develops
TimingThrough trial; rush accommodated
