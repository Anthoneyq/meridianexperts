California-Specific Practice

Matters we are regularly retained on in California.

California's statutory framework — SB800 for residential construction defect, the Calderon Act for common-interest developments, and the ongoing body of case law on standard of care — shapes how our investigations are scoped and reported. Every California engagement is planned with the applicable statute and procedural posture visible on page one of the investigation plan.

SB800 · Residential

SB800 / Right-to-Repair investigations.

Pre-suit investigation supporting notice of claim, inspection under CA Civ. Code §§ 895–945.5, and the repair-or-litigate determination. Scope aligned with the functionality standards and the statute of limitations applicable to the unit type.

Calderon · HOA

Calderon pre-litigation process.

HOA-side and developer-side investigations supporting the statutorily-mandated pre-suit protocols — including Wall & Window inspections, testing protocols, and the rebuttable-presumption construct under SB800 §896.

Wildfire · Subrogation

Wildfire origin & subrogation support.

Investigations concerning wildland-urban interface construction, ember intrusion, Chapter 7A compliance, and post-fire subrogation. Coordination with origin-and-cause investigators to build a defensible record on both the ignition and the structural response.

Seismic · Retrofit

Soft-story & seismic retrofit disputes.

Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Oakland soft-story ordinance compliance; mandatory retrofit documentation; post-earthquake first-party and subrogation matters. ASCE 41 evaluation methodology applied where appropriate.

Envelope · Coastal

Coastal envelope & stucco-over-frame.

Water intrusion investigations on coastal multifamily and hospitality projects where marine exposure, three-coat stucco, and fenestration installation intersect. ASTM E1105 testing and destructive investigation protocols designed to survive standard-of-care rebuttal.

Commercial · Code

CBC standard-of-care analysis.

California Building Code compliance and standard-of-care benchmarking at the applicable edition as of the date of construction. Title 24 energy-compliance matters considered where causation theories implicate mechanical or envelope performance.

California Authorities

Statutes and standards regularly cited.

Every California engagement benchmarks against the edition in effect on the date of construction. Historical editions of the California Building Code, Residential Code, and applicable local amendments are maintained for matters concerning work performed under prior versions.

  • CA Civ. §§ 895–945.5
    SB800 Right-to-Repair Act
    Statutory functionality standards, pre-suit notice, and repair procedure for residential construction.
  • CA Civ. §§ 1375
    Calderon Act
    Pre-litigation procedure for common-interest developments and HOA construction-defect claims.
  • CBC / CRC
    California Building & Residential Codes
    State-adopted IBC/IRC with California amendments; historical editions maintained.
  • Title 24
    California Code of Regulations — Bldg. Stds.
    Energy, accessibility, and green-building standards considered where relevant to causation.
  • Chapter 7A
    Wildland-Urban Interface Standards
    Applied in wildfire origin, ember intrusion, and post-fire subrogation analyses.
  • SF / LA / OAK
    Municipal Soft-Story Ordinances
    Mandatory retrofit compliance, documentation, and dispute analysis in covered jurisdictions.
