01 Foundational

Damages assessment.

Documented field investigation that grounds every downstream opinion. Hail, wind, wildfire, freeze, water and flood, fire, hurricane and named-storm — residential and commercial.

  • A.

    Site investigation under documented protocols — photography, sampling, measurements.

  • B.

    Damage classification by peril, building system, and date of loss.

  • C.

    Pre-existing condition analysis and wear-and-tear differentiation.

  • D.

    Chain-of-custody documentation supporting every observation.

  • E.

    Output: OEC report and Loss Analysis suitable for claim, ADR, or litigation.

02

Repair scope & construction cost.

OEC and Loss Analysis built on regional cost data, line-item documentation, and field-verified conditions. Twenty years of ground-up construction informs every line.

  • A.

    Line-item repair scope tied to specific damaged assemblies.

  • B.

    Regional construction cost analysis — labor, materials, equipment, indirect.

  • C.

    Like-kind-and-quality matching analysis where required.

  • D.

    Differentiation of policy-covered, betterment, and pre-loss work.

  • E.

    Output: defensible Loss Analysis stage-built for adjustment, appraisal, or trial.

03

Rebuttal analysis.

Structured response to opposing reports, adjuster estimates, and Loss Analysis work product. Issues identified, methodology evaluated, conclusions tested.

  • A.

    Section-by-section evaluation of opposing methodology and basis.

  • B.

    Identification of unsupported assumptions, missing investigation, scope omissions.

  • C.

    Independent re-investigation where the opposing record is incomplete.

  • D.

    Daubert-conscious framing — facts, methods, and reasoning kept separable.

  • E.

    Output: written rebuttal report and deposition support materials.

Document — Rebuttal
04

Mediation & appraisal.

Retained appraiser, umpire services, ADR support, and demonstrative materials. Built to bring matters to resolution where the file supports it.

  • A.

    Service as a party-appointed appraiser under standard policy provisions.

  • B.

    Umpire services where both parties agree.

  • C.

    Demonstrative exhibits, mediation summaries, settlement-position support.

  • D.

    Pre-mediation joint document review and rebuttal preparation.

  • E.

    Output: appraisal award, signed report, or mediation-ready binder.

Conference — Mediation
05

Causation opinions.

When the question is which peril caused which damage, the answer must trace back to documented evidence — not narrative. Differentiation by date of loss, peril, and building system.

  • A.

    Multi-peril differentiation — prior storms, new losses, ongoing failures.

  • B.

    Construction defect vs. weather peril analysis.

  • C.

    Wear-and-tear, maintenance, and pre-existing condition treatment.

  • D.

    Coordination with metallurgical, meteorological, and structural specialists.

  • E.

    Output: causation matrix and supporting opinion section in Loss Analysis.

Forensic — Causation
06

Expert testimony.

Deposition and trial across plaintiff and defense, state and federal court. The opinion held the day it was written; it should still hold under cross.

  • A.

    Rule 26 disclosures and engagement-record preparation.

  • B.

    Pre-deposition counsel consultation and document preparation.

  • C.

    Demonstrative exhibits — photo boards, diagram callouts, scope tables.

  • D.

    Daubert and qualification challenge response support.

  • E.

    Output: deposition transcript record, trial testimony, exhibit set.
