Practice

What Meridian actually does.

Independent building consulting for property damage, repair scope, and cost — for counsel and stakeholders in complex property matters.

01 The Work Eight disciplines

Meridian identifies what is damaged, what repair is supported, where estimates differ, why costs move, and what the documents actually show.

Core Analysis
01

Property Damage Assessment

Assessment of building damage as it actually presents — what was damaged, how, and what the conditions support. Documented with photographs, measurements, and notes that hold up in the record.

02

Repair Scope Analysis

What repair the building requires, grounded in construction experience — and where competing scopes diverge from the observed and documented conditions.

03

Estimate Review

Line-level review of the estimates in the file: quantities, methods, pricing assumptions, omissions, and the specific differences that drive the dispute.

04

Cost Evaluation

Why the numbers move: pricing data, market conditions, scope dependencies, and cost exposure across the positions in the matter.

Documentation & Reporting
05

Technical Reporting

Findings organized into a concise technical report — observed, documented, compared, and supported. Written for counsel preparing for mediation, negotiation, or deeper engagement.

06

Construction Observation & Documentation

Observation and documentation of building conditions and repair progress where the matter calls for an ongoing, organized record.

07

Aerial & Building Documentation

Aerial and building documentation when warranted by access, scale, or roof conditions — in service of the analysis, never as the product itself.

When the Matter Requires It
08

Litigation Support & Expert Witness Services

Where a matter proceeds: support through discovery, deposition, and testimony. Most matters resolve earlier — the work is built so that they can.

Scope IQ — File Review

Need the file understood before anything else?

A focused desk review of existing estimates, photographs, reports, and claim documents — major scope and pricing differences, organized into a concise technical summary. No site inspection. Not geographically limited.
