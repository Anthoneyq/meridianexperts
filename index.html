The Distinction

Not every consultant is built to testify.

Inspection and expert analysis are not the same discipline. The difference shows up in deposition — and on the page.

Typical Consultants

Inspection-Oriented

  • Surface-level condition reports, written for clients — not counsel.
  • Observations without traceable methodology or documented standards of care.
  • Photos and notes that don't survive chain-of-custody scrutiny.
  • Conclusions that collapse under Daubert challenge or opposing cross.
  • Limited or no deposition and trial experience.
Meridian Experts

Litigation-Oriented

  • Reports structured for admissibility — methodology, basis, and opinions clearly separated.
  • Documented root-cause analysis grounded in published standards and building science.
  • Chain-of-custody protocols for samples, photographs, and field measurements.
  • Opinions traceable to physical evidence — built to withstand Daubert inquiry.
  • Direct deposition and trial experience in construction and insurance matters.
Decision Triggers

When attorneys bring us in.

Counsel typically retains Meridian when one of the following creates risk to the case position — and an independent, defensible expert is required to address it.

  • 01

    Damages are disputed.

    Carrier estimates, policyholder estimates, or contractor proposals diverge — and an independent damages assessment is required to support the position.

  • 02

    Competing expert opinions create uncertainty.

    Conflicting reports, weak technical bases, or unsupported conclusions need a structured rebuttal grounded in evidence and methodology.

  • 03

    Repair scope or cost is challenged.

    Scope or cost positions are under attack and require an Opinion of Estimated Costs (OEC) or Loss Analysis (LA) backed by detailed work product.

  • 04

    A matter is approaching mediation, deposition, or trial.

    Disclosure deadlines, expert designation dates, and depositions require an expert prepared to testify under cross — not merely write.

  • 05

    An independent expert is needed to evaluate or rebut prior reports.

    Late-stage rebuttal engagements where the record requires methodology review, alternative-cause analysis, and disciplined opinion structure.

  • 06

    Causation is contested.

    Storm vs. wear, defect vs. maintenance, design vs. installation. Coverage and liability often turn on a clean causation opinion supported by physical evidence.

Engagement Process

A structured, defensible workflow.

Every stage is documented. Every conclusion is traceable. The process itself is built to hold up in court.

01

Conflict & Intake Review

Confidential case screening, scope definition, and engagement terms — aligned to statute-of-limitations pressure when needed.

02

Document Review

Plans, specifications, submittals, RFIs, change orders, inspection records, and prior reports — evaluated before the first site visit.

03

Field Investigation

Non-destructive and destructive testing under documented protocols. Photography, sampling, and measurements preserved under chain-of-custody.

04

Root-Cause Analysis

Evidence evaluated against building science, published standards, and the applicable standard of care at time of construction.

05

Expert Reporting

Written opinions structured for clarity and admissibility. Opinions separated from methodology. Basis disclosed. Prepared for Rule 26 disclosure.

06

Deposition & Trial Support

Ongoing counsel consultation, demonstrative preparation, deposition readiness, and expert testimony through trial.
