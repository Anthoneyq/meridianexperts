02 The Problem Before strategy

The file should make sense before it drives strategy.

Property matters arrive as thousands of pages — estimates, photographs, reports, supplements, correspondence. An estimate alone is not an answer. Counsel needs four things from that material:

A

What the building requires

The damage as observed, and the repair the conditions actually call for.

B

What the documents support

Which positions in the file hold up against the record — and which do not.

C

Where the numbers differ

Whether the gap between estimates is a scope disagreement or a pricing disagreement. They are different arguments.

D

What the dispute is about

So mediation and settlement decisions are built on the building — not the paperwork.

04 Why Meridian The independent read

Retained to be right — not to take a side.

There are firms known for writing large numbers for policyholders, and firms known for writing small numbers for carriers. Meridian is built to be neither. We find the number the building and the documents actually support — and we stand behind it, whichever side it helps.

Either side

Genuinely independent

Counsel retains us on both sides of property disputes — for carriers and for policyholders alike. We have walked inflated demands down and low estimates up. The analysis does not change with the letterhead.

The middle

The right number

Most matters do not sit at either extreme; they resolve somewhere in between. Our work is to find where, support it against the record, and give counsel a defensible number to negotiate from.

First

The first call

The most useful time to bring Meridian in is early — before positions harden. We help frame the entire matter, not just hand over an estimate, and bring in the right expertise as it is needed.
